Idea #64

Business

Transition NASA engineers into an institute for advancing solar energy technology.

Value Proposition

Not sure yet how this is represented as a business, but the idea is to invest existing resources into developing an endless supply of clean, cheap (really cheap) energy. Frankly we all appreciate Mars exploration, but our energy concerns are more immediate than visiting another planet. The only way renewable energy will REALLY advance quickly is to make it affordable for everyone is the world, so why not re-channel the greatest engineering minds we have onto that immediate objective? Hey, recently unveiled at CES was a $10 laptop… why not a $20 solar panel?

 

Customer segment

Any residence, business or municipality.

Channels

Gov’t resources currently spent on space exploration to be repurposed for this international project. Would probably have to be multi-lateral to be effective. It’s an investment, and the payback is years down the road when we all have unlimited clean energy from the sun.  

Revenue

Revenue is hard to identify with this idea. Unless we consider saving the current investment in space exploration.

 

 Idea #65

Business

Transition NASA engineers onto advancing the technology around desalinization of ocean water.

Value Proposition

This is one more that is difficult to conceive of as a business, but is there anything more important than availability of consumable fresh water? You can’t last more than a few days without it, but we are not getting to Mars in time to rehydrate.

The only way we solve the problem of droughts is to make desalinization fast and affordable, so here again why not re-channel the greatest engineering minds we have onto that immediate objective?

 

Actually, once we have the unlimited supply of cheap solar power, we can have almost unlimited fresh water. (Lonny wants his New York Times obit to thank him for his work to make fresh, clean water available)

Customer segment

Any municipality.

Channels

Gov’t resources currently spent on space exploration would be repurposed for this initiative. Like cheap solar power, this is an investment, and the payback comes when we all have essentially unlimited clean fresh water.  

Revenue

Revenue is hard to identify with this idea. But eliminating the damage from droughts, and consequential limits on agriculture, has immense value. It can be monetized somehow.
