Uber-garden: ultra efficient neighborhood-community greenhouse gardens, for profit, 100% organic, no pesticides, naturally grown plants in your neighborhood managed by an Uber type organization. maximized output, alternative energies powering the fans and lights. value proposition: expertly managed gardens for an annual fee  customer segments: people who want top grade produce from a neighborhood source

Energy

  1. Real time feedback on pricing options for wholesale fuel suppliers.  Real time pricing for fuel products at the wholesale level can be much more dynamic than it is today.  With this service, wholesale jobbers who are driving trucks could see exactly which fuel terminal is the most economical for them to lift product from given their current location, travel time, transportation costs (for their vehicles), and post market prices for the product, or mix of products, they are to load.  In this business literally hundredths of a cent count and more granular the data you can process the better off you will be.

Real time portfolio modeling program for large utilities to calculate instantaneous cost to produce.  Long term planning models are normal, but this service would take into account the price of inputs that day and the market for outputs that day, factor in labor costs to produce the product and make an instantaneous P&L by hour for a manufacturing operation.
  5. Design a room-by-room thermostat  that sense when people are in the room (or not) and runs heat or A/C in that room only accordingly.  This technology would use an innovative method of cooling that allows each room to be zoned separately using micro chillers for piping systems in the floor of each room to help control temperatures within a 7-8 degree bandwidth based on personal preference.

By leveraging social media parents with children of common interests can gain insight into what kids are viewing using an integrated application. Parents can pool resources to “block” individual pieces of content. At scale, the platform creates near-real-time curation, which is better than a technology driven solution and because the platform can operate virally, it can successfully curate at scale. Parents would pay an annual fee for the service and those who flag the most content would get paid for that effort (provided that other parents like (agree) with their choices). Additional revenue opportunities are created by redirecting the children to other content that is known family friendly with advertising.
