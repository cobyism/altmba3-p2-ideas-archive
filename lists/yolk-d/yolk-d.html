Have you ever had an idea for a startup?

By AndrewStrommeJanuary 14, 2016

Teague, Keeley, Peter, and I have 99 business plans that we are giving away to you for free. Each one is potentially the seed of something big. But this was the easy part. Tending to one and letting it grow is much harder, and more remarkable.

 

1. Sell a scale that that doesn’t tell you your weight

 

Purpose:

Help you be healthy without dreading the scale.

 

Who are the customers?

People who have decided to care about being a healthy weight.

 

Why is the work valuable to those customers?

A free companion app you create and keep habits like taking a walk instead of eating a snack in the moment rather than when you weigh yourself.

 

How do you make money?

Sell the scale.

 

2. Bundle together the search process for several weekend adventures

 

Purpose: Help outdoor enthusiasts plan their weekend adventure by showing them all of the hiking, biking, climbing, skiing they can do in a particular area.

 

Who are the customers?

Outdoor enthusiasts with an afternoon to spend.

 

Why is the work valuable to those customers?

Sometimes you care more about the place than the activity.

 

How do you make money?

Also include sponsored links to lodging, transportation, and gear.

 

3. Rent an electric car on demand for a point to point journey

 

Purpose:

Make more efficient use of capital by using it more frequently

 

Who are the customers?

Vacationers, weekend adventurers, commuters.

 

Why is the work valuable to those customers?

They pay less than they would if they had to rent the car for the entire time.

 

How do you make money?

People pay you to rent cars

 

4. An app that records the approach to crag for rock climbing.

 

Purpose:

Organize information about rock climbing to make climbing more approachable.

 

Who are the customers?

People who want to climb outdoors

 

Why is the work valuable to those customers?

It can be hard to figure out how to get to the climbs. And time spent finding the crag is time not climbing.

 

How do you make money?

Sell the user equipment that is needed in that particular location (shoes, ropes, headlamp, etc…) so that the ads are timely and useful.

 

5. Samples First

 

Purpose:

An online shopping website for essentials like shampoo and detergent that gives you free samples of a bunch of different brands. You then pick the one you like best and can set up a recurring subscription.

 

Who are the customers?

People who have change happening in their lives, like college students, new grads, new parents.

 

Why is the work valuable to those customers?

Choose once, then never worry about buying essentials again.

 

How do you make money?

Customers pay you a subscription fee once they choose their items.

 

6. A mobile web browser with a built in translation-engine that helps language learners.

 

Purpose:

Tap on a word, and see its translation first in the language of the word and then in your native language. Pair it with flashcards that are tailored to the words that you read (and look up) most commonly.

 

Who are the customers?

Language learners

 

Why is the work valuable to those customers?

It is really hard to find good material to read to help you improve.

 

How do you make money?

Allow advertisers to target ads based on the content these users read. Also have sponsored stories.

 

7. Create a cheap device for giving feedback

 

Purpose:

Encourage the world to give more feedback.

 

Who are the customers?

Businesses who care about listening to their customers

 

Why is the work valuable to those customers?

Put these everywhere, like on store shelves, in offices, bathrooms, and more. Tap, then speak the problem. Maybe create a companion app for more feedback options in the future. These businesses can now scale their ability to listen.

 

How do you make money?

Sell subscription access to the database that collects and organizes the reports making them easier to act on.

Purpose: Spaces should better support the needs of people.

 

Who are the customers?

Someone with a side project. Especially partners who have a project together.

 

Why is the work valuable to those customers?

How do you make money?

9. Build an ebook reader that has solar panels and never needs a charge.

 

Purpose:

Encourage people to read more and to share more

 

Who are the customers?

Why is the work valuable to those customers?

How do you make money?

You sell the ebook readers directly
