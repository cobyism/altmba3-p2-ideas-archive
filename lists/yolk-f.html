1 - A Community Café & Library

Value Proposition

The idea is to provide a versatile space with resources such as books and free wifi to study and all the equipment to organise activities, meetings, events...

Customer Segment

...to particularly young people/students.

Channel

We reach them through contact with youth group organisations first, word of mouth, social media campaign, flyers distributed at the nearby universities and will get them to check in (possibly by using a Facebook portal to be able to use our wifi).

Revenue

We will make money by selling coffee, drinks & snacks at all time.

We will organise one-a-month booking only dinners or brunches to benefit of the buzz and to not overwhelm ourselves and avoid wastage.

We will offer our space for rent to the public.

We will get paid from the sales revenue.

We will also look for sponsors that we can promote during our own events.

24 - Custom Storm Windows

Value Proposition

Historic homeowners have few options for replacing original storm windows. Readily available options are typically aluminum, which ruins the original character of the home.

Customer Segment

Homeowners interested in preserving the look and feel of their homes. Storm windows were typically installed on bungalows, built at the turn of the century.

Channel

Historic homeowners can be reached by marketing to historic home preservation organizations, local glass shops, and local painting companies. Glass shop owners and painters get a 10% commission on every referral.

Revenue

Initial consultation is free, resulting in a custom proposal. Pricing is based on window sizes, cover the cost of materials, plus 50% markup for profit.

Payment will be in the form of a deposit, with balance due upon completion.

Offering window repair services provides additional revenue.

Historic home owners are often in need of roofing, painting and other trade services, which pays a modest referral fee.

Challenges

Sourcing materials; especially glass.

Industry

Construction

35 - Harvesting Timber in Urban Areas

Value Proposition

When trees are cut down in urban areas, much of the wood ends up in a landfill. Many of these trees are maple, oak, pecan and other varieties that are valuable to wood craftsmen. We work with local, independant, tree service companies to harvest the wood carefully and transport it to our partners, sawmills, for processing on the way to be sold to lumber company.

Customer

Homeowners and business owners that are in need of tree services are the main customer. Lumber companies that supply craftsmen are also a customer.

Channel

Network with local tree service providers. Pay them a percentage of the profit when sold to the lumber company.

Provide them with a brochure to give to homeowners and business owners explaining the steps that will be taken from ‘cut down’ to ‘lumber yard’.

For a fee, they can have their tree made into furniture for their home.

Revenue

Sell the wood to lumber yards for market rates.

Challenges

Finding sawmills in close proximity to urban areas.

Meeting the quality standards and inventory needs of lumber companies.

Industry

Logging and Timber
