A Dollar and a Dream: Business Ideas for Bootstrappers

altMBA3 Project 2

Yolk E Cohort

99+ Business Ideas

Our Key Questions:

  1. Can this business idea be executed by a bootstrapper?
  2. Does the business idea resonate with each of us?
  3. How can the business idea generate $?
  4. Who is the perspective audience for the business idea?

  1. HomeWork Helper: Ensure your kids are understanding their school work whether the teacher has time to review or not. Create a distributed systems where parents can submit homework to reviewed by teachers/out-of-work/underworked/college student. The teachers would sign up to review and make X much per review (different rates for different levels of experience). Provide the option to upgrade to tutoring (or referral to existing system).
  2. Spray Tan in Van: The instacart of spray tans. Select time, location and shade on all on your mobile device. We come to you.
  3. Social Zapper: App that integrates with your social media (Facebook, twitter, etc) and wearable tech. Zaps you after you have spent more than a specified amount of time on social media.
  4. Investment Improver: Most people's largest investment is their home. Our consultants come by at tell you things big and small that will increase the value of you home. They make recommendations based on your budget, location, cultural needs, etc. just like a financial advisor for you the investments. Specifically relevant to interior designers who don’t have an in with the lower end of the homeowner market.
  5. Cyber Insurance: Lifelock is cool n’ all but falls short after an attack has already happen. This insurance would get everything back in order after a breach and track down everything that was compromised.
  6. Mobile Workout: Can’t get to the Gym. Bring the gym to you. The driver is also a trainer/coach.
  7. Data Only: Only data. Unlimited data. No cellular plan. Verizon and AT&T are raising the price on unlimited data or don’t offer it. Most use their phone to browse, stream music, GP, etc and cell providers want to capitalize. Not great in emergency but…. maybe 5 calls a month or 911 is free.
  8. Crowd sourced Financial plans : Usually the people that need the most help with financial planning (college students, young graduates, etc) can’t afford it. And they aren’t attractive clients for advisors. This service uses pro advisors to develop young employees financial plans.
  9. Life 101: A ridiculous number of kids graduate high school without basic skills like cooking, cleaning or financial literacy. To be a 19 year old in college and not know how to do laundry is a travesty. Create a class that can be sold to schools as a final year program that every student must go through to ensure they have the basic skills needed to live alone.
  10. Let’s Grow: Service that links up young/aspiring financial advisors with you aspiring/ workers. So they grow together. Fees would increase over time with the returns of the people they advise. Who knows, maybe your client will be the next Steve Elon Zuckerberg?
  11. Take a Ride, give a Ride/ Buy X, Give X: Similar to woot, but with a charity bent. Each week a product goes up for sale. After some many are sold one goes to charity. “200 water filters are sold one goes to ( or the value) charity” Spending with less guilt.
  12. Teaching is Easy: A site/service that gathers all the materials, books, supplies, etc. (Amazon style) in one place based on the teacher’s plan for the year/quarter. Parents can buy as needed or purchase all at once. Reminders would be sent out. "Johnny has a science fair in 4 weeks, does he have what he needs?”
  13. Mobile Mentors: Mentors are hard to find…..let’s make it easy. Build an app/service that connects people with similar interests but different skill levels. Location based.
  14. Buddies.com: Works like an online dating site for friends. Get to know people with similar interests before you hang out/go fish/ surf with them. The intent is to eventually meet up, so the awkwardness of going from forum friend to real life friend is lessened…maybe.
  15. Curated tours: Tour paris like beyonce would. Eat your way through Venice the same way Anthony Bourdain would. Explore Yosemite like John Muir. Curated interiors of city’s / museums/parks. Itineraries could be made by celebrities and experts. Filtered by interests and fitness levels.
  16. Ski-Share: A ski pass that works the same way as a time share. Collectively purchase a ski pass use at different times, maybe there have three opportunities to ski as group.
  17. My Modern Furniture: A Mash up Ikea, Legos, and West Elm: Allow users to create their own furniture with predetermined shapes (similar to lego) Then it is custom made and shipped to their house. Similar to prefab home building.
  18. Job Ebay for Artists/Fill My Canvas: Art buyers who have a custom need but have been unable to find the right size piece, or aren’t sure exactly would work in the space, can post what they are looking for. Artists can then bid/send proposal. The buyer would have have little to no control over the final work, in order to keep the work authentic and minimize the feeling of selling out. The artist community would be curated. Artists would have to apply and go through a vetting process.
  19. Nice Walls: Tags, gang graffiti, and other artistic vandalism attracts more of the same. Especially in depressed neighborhoods. (Buenos Aires, Oahu) However when a wall is thoughtfully painted it not only looks better but deters vandals.  This service/app would connect small business owners /with street artists. Street artists get the freedom to work, small business have better curb appeal.
  20. Farm Dashboard: Command central for farmers. Lots going on with farms software that helps monitor the status of the entire farm. Tagged cows, Employees, tractors, drone stuff, crop yields.
  21. Ski Buddies: Link up with locals who can show you around the mountain, based on skill level. The best runs, shortcuts and places to eat. Think uber for the mountains
  22. National Park Buddy: Link up with locals who can show you around the park, based on fitness and adventure level. The best trails, shortcuts and places to eat. Think uber for the mountains
  23. Like a Local: ARB allows you to dwell like a local. This service would allow to move about about like a local. You would pay a local for their time and they would show you around their city. Art lovers could link up with art lovers and partiers could link up with partiers. Twist on the classic buddy program. (See freetour.com - This is available in Dublin and appears to work well - Grania)
  24. Cook Nearby: App that allows you to find an amateur cook in your area that delivers meals to your home on a bi-weekly basis. They could have tiered options for monthly or weekly membership. Set menu of items per week and you choose which ones make sense for you. Kind of like Munchery, but cheaper given it’s not gourmet chefs.
  25. Ugly Onesies: Combining the Ugly Sweater with the Onesie craze. Themes based on holiday seasons, sports teams, superheroes, etc.
  26. AskHov: Hoverboard/Segway that has Siri (Apple), Alexa (Amazon) like capabilities so you can ask it anything while you ride it.
  27. HovCam: Hoverboard/Segway that has a camera built into it so you can take cool shots from different angles.
  28. Rain Burka: We wanna know, have you ever seen rain? Like real rain? The kind of rain an umbrella can’t shake a stick at? Where even if you can keep your top dry your legs are soaked? The kind of rain that laughs at hats and scoffs at ponchos? We have. So we propose developing a durable, lightweight rain burka. Ideal for sporting and outdoor events to keep you dry and clean. From head to toe.
  29. Coach in Your Pocket: App that asks you random, powerful questions as you go about your day, based on your goals and what you want to be reminded of.
  30. Employee Engagement App: App that asks people throughout the company different ‘engagement’ questions on different days so the organization can have live data constantly on how people are feeling about their work and the work environment. Cuts down on expensive employee surveys which most people hate and which are always out of date by the time HR gets, complies and shares the data.
  31. Pocket Sommelier: Scan the barcodes in the wine store, tell the app what you’re having for dinner and it choses the right pairing for you. Free app. Premium service - that you pay for - gives you the in depth scoop on the wine so you can impress friends/date or just because you want to learn more about it. Also offers virtual tastings; you buy the bottle and drink up in a video chat with a sommelier who walks you through it. All of the above can apply to craft beer and whiskey.
  32. Casting Call: Audio books are great. Not all narrator choices are great. What if you could voice cast your audio book? Offer a selection of approximately six people to choose for each main character. This is for people who love to listen to audio books. To encourage people to listen to books. This will create an extra revenue stream for the publisher. The difficult part will be the software development and marketing to the publisher who is content with one voice/one character. Fees gained from publishers. Their incentive will be to charge more for this option when selling the product. Could also work for GPS systems.
  33. Artists on Call: Local photographers/Musicians/Dj’s etc. for hire by the hour. Fees to be gained from subscribers and commissions. Develop a site to book/commission artists of all mediums easily. Make it easy to commission DJ’s, musicians at short/long notice in your area. Expose more people to the arts. Provide an income for struggling and successful artists alike. The difficult part will be building the software.
  34. Check Me: Develop a site to coordinate validated testimonials and references. This will take the hassle out of constantly providing these. A link to your personal area can be sent instead to the prospective client. This can be either publicly or privately available. Basically a YELP for people. Fees  to be gained by charging to join.
  35. Dress Me: Online stylist/wardrobe consultant. Someone who can achieve the task with a virtual meeting. they can be validated through Check Me. They can advise via Skype. Could be great stylist but not in the same city.  Basic questionnaire to commence the process.  Will be more accessible for people who don’t want to do this in person but see the necessity. Fees to be gained by subscription fees and commission and affiliate links.
  36. Original to Print: Develop an easy/less costly print creation option for artists to create prints. The demand would be great but this may fall outside bootstrapper and into VC funded startup.
  37. Art Pawn: Art is all about experience the new and seeing new things in new ways. But it can be expensive to own great art. We propose creating an art exchange or borrowing service where users could trade art with other users or borrow their pieces temporarily for a charge. Similar concept to Bagborrowsteal. Revenue can be gained by subscription/transaction.
  38. HandleItforMe: Shopping and buying stuff is fun. Taking it back when you change your mind or it doesn’t fit quite right, horrible. Remove the headache of making it right by using our pickup service to make retailer returns. Provide service by personal pickup or van pickup at a central location. Revenue created from a per item charge.
  39. MakeitforMe: Fancy handbags are cool. Unique fancy handbags...cooler. Our service would enable customised commissions of couture handbags. Similar concept to what Nike, Converse and Rayban do with their shoes and glasses. Growing trend. Premium cost per product.
  40. MyWalls: Commission your own wallcovering. Created of the spines of the books/photos of your choice. At an affordable price. Bring out the artist in everyone.
  41. Local PA: Local Personal Assistant available hourly. See Check Me. Provide free time to some and employment to others. Fees to be gained by commission.
  42. Exclusive Access: For many travelers, bringing home a unique piece of art from their destination is key to the act of remembering and recalling. We propose creating a tour/introductory service to connect artists worldwide with overseas (or even local) visitors interested in finding new pieces/works. Users choose types of art and times available, the service works with a database of local artists to set up a personalized  gallery tour of relevant art. See The Retreat business model at https://theretreat.life
  43. Stopit!: Effective cat scratching retardant. Effective being the key word. The advance would be undamaged furniture and sanity.
  44. Clipit!: Effective cat nail trimmer. One that takes the terror out of it all.
  45. HowMany?: Restaurant easy menu calorie counter. Easily accessible, app-baed information to assist with selection for those who count calories. Revenue created by app downloads or partnerships with diet programs like WW or Jenny Craig, etc.
  46. Let’s Talk: Online portal for renters in individual buildings and or cities to communicate free from Landlord intervention or monitoring. To exchange tips, alerts, activities. To assist in better decision making when moving. YELP for buildings.
  47. Accountability Partners online: Online site for people to ask for and give support for a specific task - creation, diet etc. The idea is to provide support from those qualified, see Check Me and also a sense of community. This can be done by fee or an exchange of service. Fees to be gained by subscription and commission where relevant.
  48. Mentors online: To assist entrepreneurs with new business items such as marketing, copy writing, branding etc. See 48 for the business model outline.
  49. Charity Central: An online portal that allows you to donate on a regular or random basis and apportion your donation to charities in categories you support. A single source place to donate to ALL the things you like. Revenue created for this non-profit venture as a small percentage of donations made (i.e. 1%).
  50. Social enterprise around covering orthodontic costs for kids for families with limited income. Not covered in any health plan (even if we had one!) and hugely expensive ($7000 for braces per kid). Could perhaps be started by the orthodontic community....
  51. Netflix Limits: Online service where you could put how many hours you want to spend on Netflix just before watching and the system will shut you down once you reach your limit - avoids binge-watching and feeling unproductive!
  52. 911 Research: On demand team of information gatherers who will provide references and links to all the latest research available (online, books, interviews) on any given topic and write a brief summary report saying which ones are most useful for what info and why. Special rates for students. Could be used by everyone from marketing firms, business, academia, speakers, etc.
  53. The Saucer, a rim for plates: A round rim that you can easily attach to a plate so food that is heavy in gravy/sauce (ie Indian food) doesn’t spill. The food stays on the plate so you get to eat it and it avoids getting things dirty.
  54. Did You Forget Something: Emergency Delivery Service that delivers forgotten items at last minute to your child. Forgotten lunches, homework assignments, books, sporting gear, gifts, etc. Partner with ride sharing app like uber and have a special service you can click via app and nearby drivers can bid to come get forgotten item and deliver it for you.
  55. Shadow Job Experience: Every wanted to be a teacher for a day? Every wanted to be a President for a day? How about a Forest Ranger? Our app allows you to shadow a career of your choice for a day. Premium packages can be sold at higher rates where you can include more with day events.
  56. Where Home Bodies Meet: An app that allows you to find other people in your area that are home bodies and looking to find people to go out with. As home bodies, you may not know where to look or even how to look. This app does all the hard work for you and you can find like minded homebodies to hang with when you decide to go for that occasional moment outdoors.
  57. CooksOnly: Dating app for just cooks. Try different cuisines, learn to make new recipes, and dine at the finest restaurant. Find your perfect match to spicen up your life. Pricing similar to match.com.
  58. Dating app for every sports team out there: ie Pats Fans Only (This could translate into 100s of diff apps for teams that don’t already have a site) or Star Wars Only Dating App: Dating app for just Star Wars fans.
  59. SwapCars: An app that allows you swap cars with someone so you can try something different for a weekend. Premiere class exchange - luxury cars; off-roading cars with more space; Idea is similar to swapping houses idea - can apply same model and pricing.
  60. CookieLock: A cookie jar with an automatic lock that once you take out a certain # of cookies it locks for the rest of the day so you can’t get anymore. Based on weight rather than timer.
  61. SwapWardrobe/SwapClothes: An app that allows you to shop in neighboring people’s closets for the week. Kind of converges Yerdle and Shopcloset idea into one but you get to share closets. Looking for a last minute dress to attend wedding or work party - look here before you go buy. Perhaps weekly/monthly subscription?
  62. HoodMeTwice: Sweatshirt that has a hood on both sides.
  63. Customized prefab bird homes: A mash-up of legos and birdhouses. Can be customized based on the type of bird you want to attract, your region, your house design. Customization would happen via the web or phone app.
  64. Stay on your Toes: Running with a heel strike is bad news for your hips. Even runners with the best intentions of staying on their toes will struggle when they are fatigued. A slide in sole with pressure sensors would send and electric pulse to your foot to let you know when you strike the ground incorrectly. And help you adjust.
  65. Sit Straight.: Wearable tech. Two electrodes would be placed on you back and calibrated. When you hunch or round your shoulders you would receive an electric pulse reminded you to sit, stand up straight.
  66. Get Up. Stand Up.: Most modern offices come with standing desks. Still, people sit. Get up stand up is a sensor added to your chair that would let you know how long you have been sitting and remind you to stand by intensely vibrating until you do. Sell to office furniture mfgs or individuals.
  67. Feedback /Advice from designers / Programmers / UXperts: Some folks just need basic design advice to move a project forward. But trying to hire a designer/programer/ ux can be time consuming. And you may realize you don’t need designer... you need a programer. Or the the design is fine but your user flow is bananas. This service would allow designers, programmers, experts to connect/ consult with small business. It would pay designers for their consulting time and help start a relationship with designers truly understand how they can help, and what they to make a website, app, product happen.
  68. 100% Anti-Fog goggles: The Hestra of goggles, we make google that don’t fog. And that’s it.
  69. Skateboard training wheels: When first learning how to ollie or kick turn, slow or no speed is vital. These Super soft urethane wheels help keep a board from moving while a novice is learning new tricks. They would look on different than regular wheels.
  70. Virtual Boy Scouts: Earn boy scout like badges online. “My troop is in the cloud”
  71. Smooth Transition: Children of those serving in the military usually have large gaps in their education. Enlisted families have to rely on public schools. They may go from a state that has low standards to one that has high education standards. This service would help transition kids from one school to the next and keep them on track.
  72. Personal Editor: Tons of photos? And not idea which ones are best. A skilled human goes through your photo collections, a a group of photos. and picks out the best one so you don’t have to. Under-worked photographers/story editors make up community. Premium service offerings could be tweaks to photos and printing of the best ones.
  73. Lease Skis/Snowboards: Like cars ski & boards models change yearly. We would allow people you ride the latest models without having to pay for the complete set up. Could also have stations that have your board waiting so you don’t have to travel with it. Saves time and get you on the mountain.
  74. No Face Alarm Clock: No noise, no light, no wireless signal. It just wakes you up when you want it to. Could be controlled by your phone,  allows you to sleep without your phone in the room.
  75. Hourly Rate Calculator: Tracks time spent at work and determines how much you actually work making it easier to determine how to spend you time. Would send you notifications, weekly summary.
  76. Carbon Monotinside: The scant chance that someone could get trapped in your garage with a running car and not exit alive is not a chance worth taking. Our garage door opener is connected to a carbon monoxide detector that triggers the garage to open when unsustainable levels of poison gas are detected in the garage. For any homeowner.
  77. Smart Windows: Use infrared smartphone attachment to identify air leaks by taking pictures of exterior of houses. Offer homeowners insulation services or referral to window installer.
  78. TranScribe: Working in a bilingual office can be confusing when you don’t know a word. Don’t fret, you scan the word with our digital pen to see an instant translation of it’s meaning on the build in e-ink screen so you can get the job done.
  79. 3rd Arm Leash: Walking your dog is great. Not being able to hold your coffee and mobile, and keep your dog in pocket is not. The 3rd Arm Leash enables you to securely leash your dog without having to sacrifice the use of one of your hands. Great for runners, mommies with strollers, or anyone at all.
  80. Bad Dog High Pitched Frequency Emitter for Runners: You like running, you hate being bitten by random dogs and you don’t want to kick a bitch. Instead of making those decisions, decide to buy our wearable that outputs a high-pitched sound to keep bad dogs at bay and lets you avoid run ins with loose dogs while running without physically harming the dog.
  81. Outsourced paper/test grading so teachers can focus on interaction and engagement: Teachers barely have enough time to plan lessons, attempt to engage students and deal with the unexpected. To then think that they have an extra 3 hours every night to review and assess assignments is ludicrous. What if they were able to get help with reviewing and assessing homework, get a report on students and then do the valuable work of engaging and teaching the students that need help? We propose using underworked teachers/substitutes/college students to do exactly that. Revenue would come from the school system.
  82. Peer-to-peer topic based help site for students: We all fear looking dumb. No one more than sensitive children and teens struggling with schoolwork. Let’s create an anonymous online portal where students can freely ask for help or offer help without fear of embarrassment. Site could be free to use up to a certain number of questions, be an affordable subscription service or supported by advertising.
  83. Snap pictures of food to add to calorie tracker database: Off the shelf module offered to existing calorie or health tracker apps that allow users to quickly add food to their diary and instantaneously know the caloric and nutritional values of what they are putting in their body. Revenue via app purchase or subscription model.
  84. P2P clothing size and order finder: It’s hard to find clothes that fit online because you can’t try them on in real life. Users would create profiles of their measurements, brand preferences and evaluate the fit of clothes they have (i.e. I’m 6’ 1”, 200lbs and like things with a trim fit. My Levi’s 511 34x32 fit me like a glove but don’t crush my man berries). Other users can then search for well-fitting clothing based on similar sized humans. Searches would provide offsite links to clothing that fits the stated parameters of the shopper. Money to be made via referral links to online retailers.
  85. MyHealth: Knowing what is happening with multiple healthcare/dental/eyecare/etc accounts is maddening. We want to create an online service to aggregate all healthcare/medical accounts/billing and provide clarity and assistance in understanding a complicated industry by bringing it all together in one place. Revenue could come from healthcare providers looking to make the system easier to use for patients or from a data-subscription model where providers get access to the combined data of patients and use it to inform marketing offerings or plan changes.
  86. MaybeWeShould: Location based application that recognizes who you are with and provides suggested things to do based on profiles and activity levels (i.e. you both would like this show, rent this DVD, etc). Advertising revenue for suggested things to do would fill the coffers.
  87. Random road trip generator: You’ve got wheels, you’ve got friends, you’ve got time. Use our app to define how much time you have and we’ll send you on a random road trip run through your phone based GPS. Revenue could come from app buy or in-experience advertising.
  88. Indoor Air Doctor: For something we do as much, if not more, than anything else, breathing sure isn’t high on the list of priorities of things to focus on when bettering one’s health. And the simple act of adding a new filter to your air system or adding house plants is an easy way to give yourself the best to breathe. We propose a service that comes to a users house, look at/replaces furnace filter and adds a “scientifically” determined right amount of easy-to-care for plants to improve air quality. Revenue comes from the service itself, from premium furnace filters (HEPA, etc) and from on-going plant maintenance options.
  89. Date Night: When you’ve got a job, a kid and many other responsibilities, sometimes the hardest thing to do is take time for yourself and your loved one. We propose creating an app that dramatically simplifies the process of creating the weekly or bi-weekly date night adventure by doing the work for you. Users would simply enter time available, budget and travel radius, a random date is created for you to select. Revenue would come from a percentage of the cost of the date (i.e. 5% of cost of movie tickets and average dinner at restaurant) and potentially though partnership referrals with with Care.com or similar to cover babysitting.
  90. WellPlated: Food trackers act as a diary of what you eat, but they seem to miss the opportunity to interpret that information and provide simple, positive tips on how to eat better based on what you have been eating. We propose creating a recipe app to tie in with food trackers to identify patterns and suggest healthier tweaks to unhealthy meals. For example, if you eat mac and cheese 2x a week, it would suggest that you try replacing noodles with quinoa to get X/Y/Z benefits. By also providing coupons for recommended product types, revenue could be created from from ads, links to recipe sites or even promotion of FreshDirect/healthy meal services.
  91. UpNext: Everyone’s got a Netflix queue. Not everyone knows what’s on everyone else’s queue. UpNext is an online service that allows friends compare Netflix queues to find movies to watch together.
  92. NoTextNeck: Slouching over your desk is bad for you. But so is looking straight down at the smartphone in your hand. We propose creating a posture checking app that can run in the background of your OS to monitor phone usage and position of phone screen to infer angle of sight. Once aware that you are Text Necking, app would send you an alert and provide tips and reminders to straighten up. Concerned users could purchase the app on their own or we could even sell/license it to a healthcare provider looking to reduce repetitive motion injuries and looking for innovative solutions.  
  93. Auto Update: Regular maintenance of your automobile is key to keeping it running. Regularly getting into your car and driving to the shop when nothing seems to be wrong with your vehicle? A huge pain in the ass. We propose creating a service that schedules your maintenance at the service location of your choice, comes to you to take your car in for 30/60/90k servicing and returns it to you. Revenue could come either directly from customers or by partnering with luxury auto makers as a built in service option for a premium market.
  94. ArtMeUp: Ever been to a house with nothing on the walls? Feels unlived in. While some of us have time, desire and funds to seek out great art and design our homes...some of us don’t. We propose building a service that simplifies this process by enabling users to snap pics of rooms to be filled, provide sizes of open areas, define desired color palette, and budget limits and in return get a personalized, cohesive set of unique art pieces to fill their home. Revenue comes from the service itself and could be improved by offering higher tier or limited edition pieces for an extra cost.
  95. ErgoHome: We all know the modern worker is killing their body by sitting in a desk every day, all day. But our supposition is that the home experience of a modern human isn’t much different. Slouching is slouching at work or at home. We propose designing and deploying an in-home ergonomics assessment and optimization session that looks at everything from dining tables to tv placement and more.
  96. iWater: One core piece of modern advice > Drink dat water. One core struggle of modern humans > Drinking dat water. While many of us have the ever present water bottle, the limited size of our usual flask frames the amount of water we are recommended to drink in a false manner. We see what we have but not what we don’t. We propose building a wifi connected, countertop water tank that fills up every morning with the recommended amount of clean, cool, filtered water. Throughout the day it will remind you to have a cup and feel the fluid via smartphone reminders, wearables notifications and even proximity detecting alerts that cause the tank to flash when an under-hydrated owner is near.
  97. Prescribed Prescriptions: As we age many of us find ourselves with myriad bottles of pills to take in different amounts on different days. It’s confusing, overwhelming and unnecessary. We propose offering an in-home (or pharmacy side) pill cutting and provisioning service that correctly allocates your medications, provides them to you by day/time/etc and ensures nothing is missed or amiss.
  98. Paleo Grocery Store/Convenience Store: Eating healthy can be easy. You just have to cut out the shit. Which makes going to the grocery store hard. Modern consumerism has packed the middle with pre-packaged, stabilizer saturated food products. So cut them out and make it impossible to make a bad decision.
  99. Full Cycle Pill Management: A major issue in the fight against antibiotic resistance is that many patients stop taking their medication when they feel better, not when the prescribed course of meds is done. This leads to stronger bacteria that no longer respond to current antibiotics. We propose creating a “connected” pill bottle or pack that monitors the prescription and only pays the insurance portion of a prescription claim when patient takes all pills. This puts pressure on the patient to follow through and ensures lower costs and reduced health issues for providers. Monetization would happen via licensing of the product/platform to prescription management companies.
  100. Pay as you grow, bro, a use-based gym membership:  Today’s gyms often offer everything to everyone. Weights, cardio, classes, saunas, etc. But we don’t all use all of the amenities, so should we pay for them? We propose zoning different parts of the gym and charging users different amounts based on what they use. Overhead and staff would be covered and investments could be made where interest is clear. Also offers the opportunity for members to tests of other sections for free and the gym can grow subscription revenue from there.
  101. Schoose: Incentivized shoe pricing. Customers bring in used shoes, get credit for new ones. Used ones donated and used as tax deduction or sold/donated in other countries.
  102. Pre-emptive Bill Explanation: Create a service for healthplans, utilities, cable companies, etc and actively call customers to offer help understanding their bills.
  103. Growing: Create an eco-conscious progressive baby and parental education subscription box service. Busy parents wants the best for their children. Give them healthy, brain and body developing toys that progress with age and offer parents short, actionable information on best practices for the development stage their child is at.
  104. Cord Cutters: Many people are interested in cutting the cord. Not everyone knows exactly how or what their options are. Our service would set up home pc theater/streaming boxes and connect customers to services. Revenue would come from the install and on-going support services.
  105. Honey, I’m Home!: Ever had to search around on your hands in knees outside in the snow at 4am because you didn’t know where your water main shut off valve was? Now you won’t have to with our new home owner how to intro service. We come to your house and show you the location and overview of operation of all of your major appliances and systems. Revenue on a per walkthrough basis with potential for referal revenue to local plumbers/electricians/etc.
  106. Auto transcription of patient visits with human checking. Analyze patterns in convos. Recos and insight.
  107. WellKit: When you need a bandage you need a bandage, so shouldn’t you have one? WellKit is a subscription medicine cabinet service run off IoT. We created the perfect collection of home medical objects and put them into a slick, self-contained kit. That’s connected to the internet. And knows when something is almost out. And automatically orders it for you. Initial purchase and subscription stocking revenue streams.
  108. All Night Normal Clinic: Many of us either work all day or work at night, so how is it that the services we need, including vital health care services, are only open during the day or via Emergency Rooms. We would open a normal doctor and pharmacy at night at regular rates.
  109. Video chat add on module for healthcare apps: More and more patients have smartphones with apps and more and more healthcare providers are creating apps for those smartphones. In an effort to cut cost and improve patient access, video services are being utilized. We would carve out a focus on building a video chatting service and offer it to healthcare providers, doctors and clinics for a subscription.
  110. Patient Advocate For Hire: Many of us are intimidated when going to the doctor and either don’t always know the right questions to ask, or don’t ask them out of fear. Our service would offer informed advocates who would accompany you to your appointment and ensure your opinion is being expressed and ensure you understand the information being shared with you and options in front of you. Revenue would come either from patients who purchase the service individually or from healthcare plans/providers who subscribe.
  111. Second opinion. Sometimes in life, you don’t need a friend, a parent or a therapist, you just need the opinion of a pretty sharp person who doesn’t know you. Our service would offer time-slot based advice via phone calls. Callers would talk to an impartial person. Not a therapist, a sounding board. Revenue generated per call/time-slot.
  112. GymTime time-slot based gym: Often when you don’t have an appointment, you don’t go. Our concept would offer customers hour-long slots at a gym. Customers would be limited in total and could only attend the gym during their purchased hours. Revenue from time-slot purchase, sliding scale for peak vs off hours.
